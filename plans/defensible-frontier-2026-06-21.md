# Sandy Chaos — Defensible Research Frontier Plan (2026-06-21)

**Framing:** plain physics, claim-tiered, a falsifier on every experimental claim.
No symbolic shorthand or contract numbers — this is meant to be read and executed directly.

---

## 0) The whole program in one sentence

Sandy Chaos lives or dies on a single discriminating result: **an information
advantage that is mechanism-driven, not inference-driven** — one that is *gated by
a physical regime boundary* (flow criticality, or record/entropy production) that a
pure history-only forecaster has no reason to respect. Everything below exists to
produce that one result, or to honestly falsify it.

If we can only ever show "a sensor helps you predict," the program reduces to
ordinary forecasting and we should say so. The regime-gating is the difference
between real physics and a poetic relabel.

---

## 1) Where we stand (honest ledger)

### Built and defensible
- **Subcritical wave channel.** In flow slower than its own surface-wave speed,
  ripples from a downstream obstacle travel back upstream at finite speed. The
  measured onset delay is **3.1938 vs a predicted 3.1928 (~0.03%)**, in a real
  wave solver, and the upstream information **collapses as the flow approaches
  critical**. Forward-causal, real, cleanly shown.
- **Self-correcting ledger.** A false "proper-time asymmetry" result — it measured
  affine-parameter step counts on null geodesics, where proper time is identically
  zero — was caught and demoted to review. This self-correction is the program's
  most externally legible asset right now.
- **Self-consistency seed.** The narrative-boundary coupling (a story about the
  future conditioning a present boundary; the surviving corridor is the
  self-consistent one) is a working toy of the idea in Step 4.

### Clarified this round (conceptual gains)
- The flow result is **spatial, not temporal.** "Downstream in space" is not
  "future in time." The only honest bridge is an observer that *moves with the
  flow* (Step 3).
- **The triviality trap.** Any sensor beats a blind baseline. The only meaningful
  comparison is against the **best history-only forecaster.**
- **Dead ends mapped.** Reading the future (directed photons / black-hole telegram)
  is walled off by chronology protection and the no-signaling theorems. The future
  can **constrain** the present (self-consistency) but cannot **signal** it.
- **The safe, rich direction is retrodiction.** Reading the past is allowed; a
  future observer genuinely knows more about a past event than the people living it,
  because records keep forming after the fact (the thermodynamic arrow).

### Dead — do not build
- Controllable future→past communication (no-signaling + chronology protection).
- Black-hole / wormhole "telegram" (exotic matter; can't predate the channel; the
  smooth time-looping regions are believed censored).

### Parallel cleanup (not gating)
- The complex-entropy object `Z = α + iβ` is decorative — it is the plane in
  disguise, and with `β = 1 − α` it collapses to a line, so there is no phase space
  to integrate over. Ground it in a real principle or retire it.
- Keep the rotating-spacetime (Kerr) layer **illustrative only** (already
  downscoped — correct). No downstream prose may treat it as load-bearing.

## 2) The plan (sequenced, plain physics)

### Step 1 — Define the observable *(do first; cheap; it is the gateway)*
- **Goal:** one written definition of "extractive advantage" that is *provably
  distinct* from ordinary mutual information about a present configuration. It must
  specify: (a) the target is a state the contemporaneous observer did **not** have
  access to — its own future, or a past event whose records were still incomplete at
  the time; (b) the baseline is the **strongest history-only forecaster**, never a
  blind or channel-off control; (c) a pre-registered pass/fail threshold and the
  regime-gating prediction.
- **Done when:** the definition + pre-registration is written and dated, *before*
  any new code runs.
- **Self-check / falsifier:** if a plain thermometer satisfies the definition, it is
  not sharp enough — rewrite it.
- **Why first:** without this there is no pass/fail line, and no experiment means
  anything.

### Step 2 — Retrodiction experiment *(do second; most defensible)*
- **Goal:** measure how well a *future* observer reconstructs a past event using only
  present-state records, versus the **best contemporaneous forecaster** at the time
  of the event. Control knob: the **record-/entropy-production rate.**
- **Done when:** one clean run with the retrodictive advantage plotted against
  record-production rate, with confidence intervals.
- **Falsifier (pre-register):** the advantage must **vanish** when record formation
  is suppressed (a reversible, low-dissipation regime). If it persists with no
  records forming, the effect is just inference — report it, and the retrodiction
  claim drops to "ordinary forecasting."
- **Why here:** reading the past is physics-allowed, so this validates the estimators
  and the forecaster baseline in the *safe* direction first, and yields a defensible
  result either way.

### Step 3 — Moving-observer anticipation experiment *(the name-claim)*
- **Goal:** put the observer *in the flow*, advecting toward the downstream boundary;
  target = its own local state at t+τ; baselines = (i) a channel-removed twin,
  (ii) a history-only forecaster. Measure the anticipatory advantage across the flow
  regime.
- **Done when:** one run showing the advantage either present with the predicted
  lead-time scaling, or a clean negative — both are progress.
- **Falsifier (pre-register):** the advantage must **collapse as the flow approaches
  critical** — the upstream channel physically pinches shut there, a boundary a
  forecaster has no reason to respect. If the advantage survives past criticality, it
  is coming from inference, not the channel, and the anticipation claim fails.
- **Why after Step 2:** it reuses validated machinery and faces the harder,
  forward-time direction with the measurement already trusted.

### Step 4 — Self-consistency handshake *(the rigorous home for "future influence")*
- **Goal:** model two coupled ends (a "past end" and a "future end") sharing one
  deterministic structure; show the only surviving history is the **self-consistent
  fixed point.** This formalizes what "messages from the future" actually reduce to:
  *constraint, not signal.* Compare against the existing narrative-boundary coupling.
- **Done when:** a toy where the self-consistent solution is forced, with the
  comparison artifact.
- **Falsifier (pre-register):** **no party may inject a freely chosen bit.** If the
  future end can transmit an arbitrary chosen message, the model is wrong — that
  capability is exactly what self-consistency forbids.
- **Why:** it captures the salvageable core of the black-hole intuition without
  building anything physics forbids, and connects to a lane we already have.

### Step 5 — Parallel cleanup *(non-gating; run anytime)*
- **Complex-entropy:** either derive `Z` from a real physical principle (a partition
  function, or an analytic continuation with actual content) or retire it and
  re-express one downstream result in plain `(order, disorder) ∈ ℝ²`. Record what, if
  anything, is lost.
- **Kerr:** confirm it reads as illustrative-only everywhere downstream.

## 3) Claim tiers for the whole frontier

- **Defensible now:** the subcritical channel; the self-correcting ledger;
  retrodiction-beats-contemporaneous because records grow; self-consistency is a
  paradox-free constraint.
- **Plausible, unproven:** that the retrodictive and/or anticipatory advantage is
  *sharply regime-gated* — the discriminating signature.
- **Speculative:** that any of this yields *usable* information beyond what the
  constraint itself pins down.
- **Dead:** controllable future→past signaling.

---

## 4) The single first move

Write **Step 1** — the definition + pre-registration for the **retrodiction
experiment** (target, history-only baseline, entropy-gating prediction, pass/fail
threshold), dated, before any code. Then run **Step 2.**
