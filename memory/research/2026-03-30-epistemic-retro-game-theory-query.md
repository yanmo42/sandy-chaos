# Query: Anchor epistemic retro-influence in game theory literature

## Question
How does the Sandy Chaos concept of "epistemic retro-influence" (downstream boundary conditions influencing upstream states via forward propagation of structural information, without retrocausality) connect to established game theory concepts like signaling games, Bayesian persuasion, forward induction, and epistemic game theory?

## Scope
- Literature search on game theory concepts involving belief updating based on anticipated future states/actions
- Mapping between epistemic retro-influence formalization and game-theoretic models
- Identification of existing formal results that can ground/validate the Sandy Chaos framework
- Explicit bridge: show how epistemic retro-influence can be viewed as a special case or extension of known game-theoretic phenomena

## Claim tier
- **Defensible:** Direct citations showing conceptual parallels between epistemic retro-influence and specific game theory constructs
- **Plausible:** Formal mapping that shows epistemic retro-influence as instance of broader game-theoretic class
- **Speculative:** Novel extensions or synthesis beyond existing literature

## Failure condition
- No clear game-theoretic analogues found after examining at least 10 relevant papers
- Mapping requires significant distortion of either epistemic retro-influence or game theory concepts
- Existing literature directly contradicts core premises of epistemic retro-influence
