## 1) Plain-language definition

**Tempo shift** changes the local rate, weight, or timing of a system.

**Phase sift** selects which trajectories remain coherent enough to matter.

The useful Sandy Chaos interpretation is:

> intelligence improves when a system can filter noisy local possibilities into fewer, more coherent action paths without pretending it knows the whole future.

The field image is a cube-grid of local state cells. Each cell contains possible motion vectors. A sift layer passes through the grid, suppressing unstable paths and preserving trajectories that match rhythm, cost, stability, and intent.

## 2) Symbolic diagram grammar

The preferred symbolic illustration is a **field-sieve diagram**:

- **Wireframe cube grid:** local Hamiltonian / phase-space cells.
- **Silver arrows:** raw motion vectors and possible impulses.
- **Warped grid lines:** tempo shift, local timing, or effective gravity-weight modulation.
- **Gold comb / prism / sieve:** phase sift operator.
- **Red-orange fuzz:** unstable, noisy, or high-dissipation paths.
- **Gold-white threads:** coherent selected trajectories.
- **Green-cyan body halo:** adaptive physical stability or readiness buff.
- **Branching linework above the head:** cognition as pruned prediction, not magic omniscience.

Compact icon:

> a metronome inside a wireframe cube, crossed by a sieve-comb, resolving chaotic arrows into one clean spiral thread.

This grammar belongs in symbolic maps and concept art. It is not a substitute for a mechanism.

## 3) Architecture mapping

### 3.1 Energistics

In energistics terms, phase sift is a selection operator over admissible trajectories:

$$
S_\phi : \Gamma(M) \rightarrow \Gamma_{coherent}(M)
$$

where:

- $M$ is the declared state space,
- $\Gamma(M)$ is the candidate trajectory family,
- $\Gamma_{coherent}(M)$ is the subset that passes stability, cost, and constraint filters.

The operator is only mechanism-level if the model declares:

- the state space,
- the candidate trajectory generator,
- the scoring / filtering criteria,
- energy or information costs,
- and failure conditions.

### 3.2 Cognitive Tempo Orchestration

For CTO, phase sift maps cleanly onto attention and readiness scaffolding:

- suppress distracting action branches,
- preserve a few tractable next actions,
- reduce noisy switching,
- keep intervention pressure bounded and inspectable.

The user remains the final initiator. Phase sift shapes the local possibility landscape; it does not author the action.

### 3.3 Temporal Predictive Processing

For temporal predictive processing, phase sift is a pruning lens:

- candidate futures are not treated as real future access,
- prediction branches are ranked by current evidence, cost, and update stability,
- the system improves by rejecting low-signal branches earlier.

The intelligence gain is therefore a signal-to-noise gain, not an unbounded IQ claim.

### 3.4 Symbolic Maps

As a symbolic operator, phase sift should be stored as:

- operator: `phase-sift`
- fantasy: coherent path selection under noisy possibility pressure
- failure mode: over-filtering, brittle precision, loss of adaptive exploration
- bridgeable domains: attention design, control corridors, trajectory scoring, symbolic composition

## 4) Physical-buff translation layer

In ability-language terms, the same operator yields different surfaces:

- **Self:** balance, timing, impact routing, traction, jump vector selection, fatigue economy.
- **Environment:** local friction pockets, falling-object denial, projectile curve edits, pressure lanes, terrain softening / hardening.
- **Cognition:** attention gates, sensory prioritization, prediction pruning, reduced switching noise.

In Sandy Chaos terms, these are three readings of one pattern:

> restrict the local trajectory family until the useful path becomes cheaper than the noisy alternatives.

---

## 5) Failure conditions

The concept fails or must be downscoped if:

1. "phase sift" becomes a poetic label without declared variables.
2. gravity language is used as literal mechanism without scale analysis.
3. the filter removes exploration and produces brittle behavior under environmental change.
4. the model cannot outperform a simpler salience or trajectory-ranking baseline.
5. symbolic-map records cannot distinguish this operator from generic focus, attention, or control.
6. cognitive claims drift into hidden coercion or forced action.

---

## 6) Smallest useful next test

Build a toy trajectory-sieve demo:

1. generate a 2D or 3D field of candidate paths,
2. score each path by curvature, cost, obstacle contact, and goal progress,
3. render rejected paths as noisy red-orange fragments,
4. render selected paths as gold-white coherent threads,
5. compare selected-path success against a naive shortest-path or random baseline.

If the toy demo does not improve clarity or performance over the baseline, keep phase sift as symbolic vocabulary only.

---

## 7) Summary

Phase sift is the disciplined version of "tempo sift":

> do not merely change the tempo of the field; filter the nearby trajectory space so coherent paths survive and noisy branches decay.

Its defensible core is signal-to-noise control over action and prediction paths.
Its speculative edge is Hamiltonian / gravitational imagery.
Its repo value is as a bridge between symbolic maps, energistics, cognitive tempo orchestration, and future visual demos.
